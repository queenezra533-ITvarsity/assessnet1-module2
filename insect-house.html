But the abundance of insects is not always a reason to worry. More often, it is actually a positive sign of an ecologically balanced environment. Did you know that out of all known insects only one to three percent do harm to our produce? The remaining 97 to 99 percent perform crucial services for the ecosystem. Some insects are responsible for the pollination of our food crops and flowers; some are part of the food chain, providing nutrition for bats and birds; and other decompose organic matter, which is important for maintaining soil fertility.
